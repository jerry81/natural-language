Lesson: Simple Phrases in German

In this lesson, we will learn and practice simple phrases that will be useful in everyday conversations.

Wie geht es Ihnen? - How are you? (formal)

This phrase is used to ask someone how they are doing in a formal setting.
Mir geht es gut. - I'm doing well.

This is a common response when someone asks how you are. It indicates that you are feeling good or well.
Sprechen Sie Englisch? - Do you speak English?

This phrase is used to inquire if someone can speak English. It can be helpful when you need to communicate in English.
Entschuldigung, mein Deutsch ist nicht so gut. - Excuse me, my German is not very good.

If you are struggling with the language or need to apologize for your limited German proficiency, this phrase can come in handy.
Let's expand on these phrases with some additional content:

Guten Tag! Wie geht es Ihnen?

Good day! How are you? (formal)
This is a polite greeting followed by the question "How are you?" in a formal context.
Es freut mich, Sie kennenzulernen.

It's nice to meet you. (formal)
Use this phrase when you want to express pleasure in meeting someone for the first time in a formal setting.
Ja, ich spreche ein wenig Deutsch.

Yes, I speak a little German.
This is a response to the question "Sprechen Sie Deutsch?" to indicate that you have some knowledge of the language.
Wo ist die nächste U-Bahn-Station?

Where is the nearest subway station?
Use this phrase when you need directions to the nearest subway station or when asking for public transportation guidance.
Now, let's move on to a short conversation exercise:

Conversation:
Person A: Guten Tag! Wie geht es Ihnen?
Person B: Guten Tag! Mir geht es gut, danke. Und Ihnen?
Person A: Mir geht es auch gut, danke. Sprechen Sie Englisch?
Person B: Ja, ich spreche ein wenig Deutsch.
Person A: Das ist gut. Entschuldigung, mein Deutsch ist nicht so gut.
Person B: Kein Problem. Ich kann Ihnen helfen, wenn Sie möchten.
